Press Release on the Appointment of
CBRT Deputy Governors
31 August 2019, No: 2019-35
With a decree published in the Official Gazette No. 30874 dated 31 August 2019, Murat Çetinkaya
and Dr. Oğuzhan Özbaş have been appointed as CBRT deputy governors to the vacant positions.
Murat Çetinkaya
Born in 1970, Murat Çetinkaya graduated from TED Ankara College in 1988 and earned his B.S.
degree from the Middle East Technical University, Department of Metallurgical and Materials
Engineering in 1992. He received his M.A. degree from Marmara University Banking and
Insurance Institute, Banking Department.
Murat Çetinkaya started his career at T.C. Ziraat Bankası A.Ş. Fund Management Department, and
then worked at the treasury departments of Tekstilbank A.Ş. and Diler Yatırım Bankası A.Ş. He
served as the CEO and Board member of Halk Yatırım Menkul Değerler A.Ş., and as the founding
Deputy Chairperson of Halk Portföy Yönetimi AŞ (portfolio management company) between 2010
and 2017. Mr. Çetinkaya joined Borsa Istanbul Group in June 2017 and acted as Deputy
Chairperson and CEO. He also served as the Chairman of Takasbank A.Ş. During his term of office
at Borsa Istanbul, Mr. Çetinkaya focused on Turkish lira financial architecture and share market
structuring.
Dr. Oğuzhan Özbaş

Born in 1971, Oğuzhan Özbaş graduated from Boğaziçi University, Department of Industrial
Engineering in 1993. Pursuing his education in the US, Mr. Özbaş earned his M.S. degree in
Industrial Administration by ranking first from Carnegie Mellon University in 1995.
Starting his professional career in the Ford Motor Company, Mr. Özbaş served at the Treasury
Department of the Company until 1998. He completed his Ph.D. in Financial Economics in
Massachusetts Institute of Technoloy (MIT) between 1998 and 2002. Mr. Özbaş joined the
University of Southern California in 2002 as Assistant Professor. He lectured in undergraduate,
graduate and post-graduate programs, and conducted research on corporate finance, real
investment, resource allocation and transfer, capital market regulation and the economic theory
of organizations.
